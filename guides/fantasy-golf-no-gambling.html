The Problem: Daily Fantasy Golf Isn't Available Everywhere

Daily fantasy sports platforms like DraftKings, FanDuel, Underdog Fantasy, and Chirp Golf require real-money entry fees and pay out cash prizes. This classifies them as paid contests — and in many US states, this type of activity is either banned outright or hasn't been explicitly legalized.

The result: if you live in one of the 18 states where DraftKings is unavailable, you can't use those platforms for fantasy golf. The same applies to Chirp Golf, which is only available in roughly 20 states, and Underdog Fantasy, which has its own state restrictions.

But that doesn't mean you can't play fantasy golf. Non-gambling fantasy golf apps have no geographic restrictions because they don't involve real money.
